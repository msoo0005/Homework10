Renewable Energy

Renewable VS. Non-Renewable

As climate change and carbon emissions are becoming a prevalent issue of the world, many countries are looking to renewable energy to reduce the impact. Over 60% of the world's electricity comes from fossil fuels and only around 25% comes from renewable energy sources.

Renewable Energy Across the World

Across the world, different countries are producing different amount of renewable energy and non-renewable energy. In 2018, the country with the highest proportion of renewable energy production is Somalia with 94.88% and the lowest country is Saudi Arabi with 0.02%.

World Energy Rankings

In terms of pure electricity generation output in 2018, Asia is the fastest growing and has the highest electricity generation of approximately 3,500,000 GWh mostly due to the growth of renewable energy use in China.

Growth of Renewable Energy

Renewable Energy Sources Act (EEG)

The Renewable Energy Sources Act, or 'EEG', is a scheme by world governements to encourage additional electricity generation from renewable energy sources. It first began in 2000, with ammendments throughout the years, the most notable being EEG 2009 where ammendments such as a higher feed-in tariff for wind energy and solar energy caused a spike in renewable energy use.

Renewable Energy Investment Growth

With the issue of climate change, it is becoming more and more important to companies and the public to be environmentally friendly. Hence, more money is being invested into research of renewable energy sources and its implementation. The first most notable spike in investment money is in 2009 and continues to grow with the majority of investments in Hydropower, Solar and Wind energy sources.
